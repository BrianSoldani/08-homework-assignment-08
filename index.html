My Journey:
From a very early age I realized I loved being in business. When I was young, I used to climb up in the walnut trees around the park and cut down mistletoe. I would break it into small batches and tie red ribbon around the stems, then package it, and sell it door to door around the neighborhood. It was a great way to raise funds for buying holiday gifts or adding to my candy supply.

As I enterd college I already knew I wanted to major in business. The world was big and I was thinking big so studying International Business seemed like the only logical choice for me. After college I found myself working for one of the biggest international food distributors in California, in what I thought was the best career ever. I was selling gourmet food and specialty items to grocery stores, restaurants, and bakeries. I relocated often, up and down the state, and truly enjoyed helping the customers I interacted with build their businesses by offering them my products and service.

When I made it to San Jose, I took a chance and applied to a little startup called Yahoo. As employee #2095 I spent many years there leading teams and driving successful products. I'm often told that I'm an awesome teammate, great business partner, and being the reason an organization stands out within the company.
